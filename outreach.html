Outreach

The Diwali Festival

Team 7610 attended the Diwali Festival to showcase many aspects of FIRST and robotics as a whole, including the process of building a robot itself, the different divisions within FIRST, and the concept of Gracious Professionalism.

The Intuitive Surgical Open House

On October 21, Cupertino Robotics attended the Intuitive Surgical Open House, which generously invited FIRST robotics members to rotate through three interactive stations. Students used and explored several state-of-the-art surgical robots, attended presentations delivered by Surgical Intuitive executives, detailing their own education and career paths before opening up for questions. This experience not only introduced students to cutting-edge technology, but it also allowed them to witness real-world applications of robotics in the industry. Learning how developing technology can improve daily life and catching a glimpse of robots in action ensured that this experience was one to remember.

The AI Workshop Series

To create a thirst for learning within Cupertino Robotics, the club decided to host many new workshop series’s. One of these many workshops’s is the AI Workshop held by two of our very own FTC members - Shashank Venkatramani and Pranav Eranki . These AI workshops help students interested in Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence a chance to try it out and learn a lot. Over the course of the workshop the final goal is to create a functioning machine learning app. Even though this workshop has just started it has helped our team a lot by helping members of our team understand machine learning, and image recognition to build functioning applications.

The Fall Festival

As a club, we demonstrated our robots from FRC, FTC and FLL and spread the influence of robotics throughout the community. From a decorative booth, great visuals, to an awesome mascot, the Fall Festival this year was a major success. One of the biggest aspects of CHS robotics is the FTC team and at the Fall Festival, we proudly presented our experiences in these teams to the community. We held numerous talks demonstrating and explaining what we do in FTC. Over the years, our FTC team has grown from a small team to numerous successful teams and sharing this with the community was truly thrilling. The audience was extremely thrilled to learn about the different aspects of our club and the way it worked.

FUHSD Call-A-Thon

On October 17th a group of students from Cupertino Robotics went to Fremont High school to help out at a call-a-thon for raising money for Cupertino Robotics and helping our school and other after school activities function smoothly with enough funding to fund them. We drove down to Fremont High School after the school day was over and met lots of new people with the sole purpose of helping raise money by calling respectful FUHSD parents and requesting them to donate money for the cause of helping us raise money.

Google Brain Speaker

At Cupertino high school every year one of our past presidents who currently works at google - Eric Jiang and Alexsandra Faust, comes to Cupertino High School to talk to us about machine learning at google and how its implicated. They came at lunch to give us a lecture about their job and how they imply values of gracious professionalism and coopertition in the workplace this helped us understand implications of the values and failure in real life.

FLL Competition

On November 11th, Cupertino Robotics decided to host the annual FLL Competition. This year, the mentors decided to let as many people as possible compete, so, they decided to host a double competition. This FLL Competition gave many FTC teams a chance to view how younger people demonstrate Gracious Professionalism and what it means to different Generations. This competition helped all the members of our team understand the various core values and their implications in real life. We were also able to learn a lot about real life problems and space and wonderful solutions displayed by the young middle and elementary school children.
